How HealthRadar works

Each “radar” focuses on a specific type of NHS service – starting with dentists – and continuously checks availability so you don’t have to.

1. Choose the care you need

Start with NHS dentists via DentistRadar. Soon, you’ll be able to track GPs, A&E, and other services from the same place.

2. Set your postcode & radius

Tell HealthRadar where you are and how far you’re willing to travel. Your radar quietly watches for changes in availability.

3. Get alerts when something opens up

When a practice shows as accepting new patients, you get an alert – so you can act quickly before places fill up.

Frequently asked questions

Is HealthRadar an NHS service?

No. HealthRadar is an independent service that uses publicly available information (like NHS listings) to help people find access faster. We don’t represent the NHS or any government body.

How does DentistRadar fit into HealthRadar?

DentistRadar is the first live radar under the HealthRadar umbrella, focused purely on NHS dentist access. Over time, everything will sit under a unified HealthRadar experience.

Will you cover GPs, A&E and other services?

Yes – that’s the plan. GPRadar is the next radar on our roadmap, followed by additional access tools where people consistently struggle to find NHS care.

How will subscriptions work?

The goal is simple: one HealthRadar Plus subscription that unlocks multiple postcodes, faster alerts and early access across all radars. Pricing will stay transparent and fair.
